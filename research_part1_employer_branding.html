1. Polish Retail Market — Employer Overview

The Polish retail market is one of the largest in Central and Eastern Europe, dominated by a combination of global giants and fast-growing domestic players. In 2024, total retail revenues exceeded 600 billion PLN. The sector directly employs more than 800,000 people, making it one of the most critical employment pillars in the country.

6. Key Trends Shaping Retail Employer Branding in Poland

The 'Retailer as Tech Company' narrative

The most sophisticated Polish retail employers (Żabka, LPP, Allegro) invest in positioning themselves as technology companies that run stores. Żabka's autonomous Nano stores, LPP's logistics AI systems and Allegro's marketplace algorithms provide genuine content for this narrative — opening a completely different talent pool.

ESG as employer brand currency

ESG credentials are increasingly important employer brand assets, especially for candidates under 35. Żabka's Platinum EcoVadis, Biedronka's CSRD compliance, Decathlon's EDGE certification and IKEA's circular business model are being used as talent attraction tools.

Salary transparency

Poland's adoption of the European Pay Transparency Directive (full implementation required in 2026) is accelerating market change. Leading retailers like Biedronka are proactively publishing salary information. Companies resisting transparency risk appearing defensive, while those that lead the way gain an employer brand premium.

The recognition deficit — the silent retention risk

The Randstad 2025 report identifies recognition as the main unmeasured driver of disengagement. For retail, where front-line managers supervise large teams with limited time, creating systematic recognition programmes represents a significant opportunity to reduce turnover without salary increases.
